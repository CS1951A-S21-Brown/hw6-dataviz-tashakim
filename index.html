Written Questions

1. Describe how your dashboard answers the questions presented.

The first bar plot displays the top 10 video games of all time, because the games were sorted by descending order of how much global sales (in million US dollars) were made from each game. That is, Wii Sports raised the highest global sales, 82 million USD, across all given years, then Super Mario Bros raised the second-highest global sales, 40 million USD, across all given years, etc.
The second pie chart answers which genre is most popular, by showing the total sales per genre (Action, Sports, Shooter, ...) broken down for each region -- which can be toggled and changed by clicking on each of the region buttons 'North America', 'Europe', 'Japan', and 'Other'.
Finally, the third question is answered by graph 3, which displays the top publisher, which is represented by each color explained in the legend (EA vs. Nintendo vs. Activision vs. Take-Two vs. THQ) for each video game genre (labeled on the x-axis).
2. List 3 reasons why D3 was helpful and improved your visualization.

(I) D3 was helpful because it is a library that focuses on data, so it was a specialized tool for putting together aesthetic data visualizations.
(II) Since D3 is lightweight and works directly with web standards, I found D3.js to be extremely fast, and worked well with large datasets that we had.
(III) D3 works with web standards including HTML, CSS, and SVG, so there was no new learning required to work on D3, and I didn't need to research on other technology or plugin other than a browser, which improved my efficiency in creating visualizations.
3. List 3 reasons why D3 would not be the best tool for creating a visualization.

(I) It could sometimes be time-inefficient to generate visualizations in D3 because it doesn't generate predetermined visualizations for you, since there are alternative sources that create quick prebuilt charts for you.
(II) D3 doesn't support all old browsers like IE8 and below. So this tool would not be accessible for everyone, and if you wanted to use SVG and CSS3 Transitions in particular, you would require a modern browser.
(III) You cannot conceal your original data easily using D3. Therefore, in the case you are using privacy-sensitive data or data you don't want to share with the public, it would not be a good idea to use D3 to create visualizations.



Accessible Data Visualization

1. Evaluate the accessibility of your dashboard based on the readings in the “Before you begin” section. What kinds of users might find this dashboard accessible and who might have more difficulty? What additional actions might you take to make this dashboard more accessible to all audiences? Your response should refer to at least one of the readings and be about 1 paragraph.

According to the first article - especially the 'design equivalent experiences for all your readers' section, I think that users with visual impairments or low vision, users with epilepsy, ADHD, cognitive disabilities etc. may have more difficulty in viewing the dashboard. This is because people with visual impairments may find it difficult to distinguish color distinctions, or may have difficulty reading small texts on the graphs. Additionally, epilepsy could be triggered by flashing colors and images, and people with ADHD or cognitive disabilities may find it difficult to interpret or follow the information, or may be distracted by the layout. Some actions we could take to make the dashboard more accessible to all audiences is by (i) using high-contrast colors, (ii) using large, clear, easy-to-read font, (iii) giving users the control of animated features (so that there are no unexpected flashes or animated objects), (iv) providing additional descriptions on how to navigate the features, (v) using ARIA landmarks to help keyboard navigation if users are making use of screen readers or keyboard etc.
2. Reflect on the stages of your design and implementation process when you could have taken steps to make your dashboard more accessible to all audiences. What are some factors that kept you from taking these steps? (a few sentences)

I could have made a more careful choice in colors, those that give much more color-contrast; However, the factors that made it difficult hence kept me from taking these steps were that (i) it would have taken a lot more time and effort to manually pick out and insert colors that have high contrast, compared to opting for the default color schemes that are pre-provided to us by D3.js, and (ii) there were a lot of different colors (up to 12 distinct colors) which made it difficult to select colors that contrasted each of each other.

Another stage of my design that I could have changed to make the dashboard more accessible to all audiences is using larger font for the labels on the graphs. What made this challenging is that due to the width of the graph and length of words that needed to be displayed, it was hard to make the font larger because they would start overlapping. To make this NOT overlap, I would have had to chop the graphs in half and display them separately, which does not help in gaining the larger picture on how each of the columns compare to each other.
